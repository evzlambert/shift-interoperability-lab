## Instructions

This reflection paper is completed **individually** after the group debrief discussion. It should be 1–2 pages (approximately 400–600 words) and submitted as directed by your instructor.

Your goal is to connect what you experienced today — the technical problems, the frustrations, the failed queries — to real-world consequences in healthcare. Think beyond the exercise. Think about what this means for actual patients, organizations, and systems.

---

## Prompt

**Based on today's interoperability lab, answer the following three questions. Write in full sentences and paragraphs — not bullet points.**

---

### Question 1 — The Technical and Organizational Challenge

Describe the single most significant data problem your group encountered today. Be specific: what was the field, what was wrong with it, and what did you have to do to fix it (if you could fix it at all)?

Then explain: was this primarily a **technical** problem (a format, encoding, or system issue) or an **organizational** problem (a lack of agreement, governance, or shared standards)? Could it have been prevented, and by whom?

*Hint: Use your Data Quality Worksheet and Interoperability Challenge Log to ground your answer in specifics.*

&nbsp;

---

### Question 2 — Impact on Decision-Making and Operations

Imagine you are a senior leader — a Chief Information Officer, Chief Quality Officer, or regional health authority director. The data problems you experienced today are happening continuously across your organization.

Choose **one** of the following scenarios and explain how the interoperability failures from today's exercise would affect real decision-making in that role:

- A hospital board asking: "How are our maternity outcomes this year compared to last year?"
- A regional health authority asking: "How many patients in our region developed complications after home births?"
- A CIO deciding between two EHR vendors — one uses open standards, one uses a proprietary format
- A compliance officer asked to produce a complete record of one patient's data under GDPR

What information would be missing, unreliable, or unusable? What would you do about it?

&nbsp;

---

### Question 3 — Patient Safety and Cost

Every data problem you encountered today has a patient on the other side of it. Describe one specific scenario from today's exercise where a data quality or interoperability failure could directly harm a patient or create significant financial cost.

Be concrete: name the data element, describe the failure, and trace the chain of events that leads to the harm or cost. Then propose the single most impactful change — technical, organizational, or regulatory — that would break that chain.

*Examples to draw from: the truncated anti-Kell antibody result, the methyldopa dose discrepancy, Baby Louise's records submitted under her mother's name, the duplicate insurance claim.*
